Resolving this dispute

You should try to solve the dispute directly with the person or organisation before making a claim by:

  • speaking to them (send an email or letter if you want a record)
  • telling them what you believe they owe you and why.
  • explaining that if you can't reach an agreement (such as a timetable for paying the money) you'll make a legal money claim.
  • telling them that they might have to pay the full amount as well as fees and other costs if they ‘defend the claim’, and that they could get a County Court Judgment (CCJ).

It might count against you in court if you don’t try to solve the dispute with the person or organisation directly before you make an official claim.
