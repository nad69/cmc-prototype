Do you owe the money claimed?

You could get a County Court Judgment (CCJ) in all cases.

Find out when you could get a CCJ

Admit all of the claim

If you admit all of the claim is fair you can contact the claimant yourself and pay the money. Ask them to let the court know when they have your payment.

The claimant can ask for a County Court Judgment (CCJ) to be made against you if you admit the claim. If they ask for a CCJ to be made you against you then you can apply for a:

  • certificate of cancellation if you pay the debt within 28 days
  • certificate of satisfaction if you pay the debt after 28 days

Admit part of the claim

If you think the claim is too high you can offer the amount you think you owe as part of your defence. You’ll get the chance to explain why your offer is the amount you believe you owe.

The claimant can ask for a County Court Judgment (CCJ) to be made against you if you part admit the claim. If they ask for a CCJ to be made you against you then you can apply for a:

  • certificate of cancellation if you pay the debt within 28 days
  • certificate of satisfaction if you pay the debt after 28 days

Reject the claim

If you don't accept you owe the amount of money claimed you can prepare and submit your defence online. You might have to go to a hearing - it can take up to 7 months to get a hearing date.

If you reject the claim and lose your case, you’ll get a County Court Judgment (CCJ) ordering you to pay the full claim amount and any costs. Details of the CCJ will be entered on the Register of Judgments, Orders and Fines for 6 years.
